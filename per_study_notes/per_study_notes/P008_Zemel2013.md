## 6. Evidence snippets

### ENV snippet

They motivate fair classification as necessary for high-stakes automated decisions (insurance, policing, advertising, loans, healthcare, admissions) and note debates among policy-makers/lawyers; fairness goals include avoiding undue bias against protected subgroups.

### DES snippet

They formalize fairness via an optimization that learns a sanitized intermediate representation Z that encodes X while obfuscating protected membership S, enforcing group fairness (statistical parity constraint on prototype assignment) and encouraging individual fairness via distance-based mapping; any classifier can be applied to Z.

### SOC snippet

They propose a two-party structure (impartial party/regulator learns fair mapping to representations; vendors build classifiers on representations) to separate fairness enforcement from utility optimization, but do not specify operational governance beyond this conceptual division.

### Specification snippet

Group fairness via statistical parity in representations: P(Z=k\|x∈X⁺)=P(Z=k\|x∈X⁻) ∀k, enforced by Lz = Σ_k \|M_k⁺ − M_k⁻\| where M_k⁺, M_k⁻ are average prototype-assignment probabilities for protected vs unprotected groups.

### Validation snippet

They evaluate LFR against baselines on three datasets and show discrimination can be pushed low while maintaining accuracy, with additional analysis of individual fairness consistency and leakage of S from Z.

## 7. Gap summary

### One-sentence gap summary

Provides an explicit, testable operationalization of fair representations that obfuscate protected membership while preserving predictive information and supports accuracy–fairness trade-offs via optimization, but does not specify acceptance thresholds or post-deployment monitoring/revision mechanisms to maintain fairness over time.

## 9. QA notes

### Reviewer coding note

Core contribution is a learned intermediate representation approach (LFR) that simultaneously targets group fairness (statistical parity) and individual fairness (consistency via distance-based mapping), with an explicit multi-objective loss (parity + reconstruction + prediction) and empirical comparisons to massaging and prejudice-regularized logistic regression. Does not address deployment monitoring or revision; fairness treated as an offline optimization with hyperparameter trade-offs rather than explicit acceptance thresholds or governance triggers.
